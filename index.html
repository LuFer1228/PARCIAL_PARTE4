IMAGINE OF DRAGONS

magine Dragons is an American pop-rock band formed in 2008 in Las Vegas, Nevada. The group, led by frontman Dan Reynolds, gained widespread recognition with their debut album Night Visions (2012), featuring hit singles like "Radioactive" and "It's Time." Known for their unique blend of pop, rock, and electronic elements, Imagine Dragons became one of the most commercially successful bands of the 2010s.

Their music often explores themes of personal struggles, self-reflection, and overcoming adversity. The band continues to release new music and remains a dominant force in modern pop-rock.

QUEEN

Queen is a legendary British rock band formed in 1970, known for their eclectic sound, theatrical performances, and genre-blending music. The classic lineup featured Freddie Mercury (lead vocals and piano), Brian May (guitar), John Deacon (bass), and Roger Taylor (drums). The band became iconic for its fusion of rock, opera, and pop, creating a unique and influential sound.

Their breakthrough came with the album A Night at the Opera (1975), featuring the groundbreaking single "Bohemian Rhapsody," which became one of the most famous rock songs of all time. Known for its complex structure and operatic elements, it helped establish Queen as musical innovators.

FIVE SECOND SUMMER

5 Seconds of Summer (5SOS) is an Australian pop-rock band formed in 2011, originally gaining fame through YouTube by covering popular songs. The group consists of Luke Hemmings (lead vocals, guitar), Michael Clifford (guitar, vocals), Calum Hood (bass, vocals), and Ashton Irwin (drums, vocals). They first caught the public’s eye when they toured with One Direction in 2013, which helped them build a large international fan base. Their self-titled debut album, 5 Seconds of Summer (2014), was a commercial success, featuring hits like "She Looks So Perfect" and "Amnesia." Known for blending pop-punk with catchy pop hooks, they quickly became popular with younger audiences. Their second album, Sounds Good Feels Good (2015), continued their success with a more mature sound, including tracks like "She's Kinda Hot" and "Jet Black Heart."

ADELE

Adele is a British singer-songwriter known for her soulful voice and deeply emotional ballads. Born Adele Laurie Blue Adkins in 1988, she rose to prominence with her debut album 19 in 2008, which featured hits like "Chasing Pavements" and showcased her powerful vocals and heartfelt lyrics. Her breakthrough came with her second album, 21 (2011), which became one of the best-selling albums of all time. The album's singles, including "Rolling in the Deep," "Someone Like You," and "Set Fire to the Rain," resonated globally, earning her numerous awards, including multiple Grammys. The album's success was fueled by themes of heartbreak, love, and loss, delivered with Adele’s signature emotional intensity.

SABRINA CARPENTER

Sabrina Carpenter is an American singer, songwriter, and actress who gained recognition both in the music industry and on television. Born in 1999, Carpenter first rose to fame through her acting role as Maya Hart in the Disney Channel series Girl Meets World (2014–2017), a spin-off of the popular 90s show Boy Meets World. Her performance on the show helped her gain a dedicated fan base. In addition to acting, Sabrina Carpenter has built a successful music career. She released her debut album, Eyes Wide Open, in 2015, showcasing her pop-driven sound. Her music blends elements of pop, R&B, and electronic beats, and she often explores themes of youth, love, and self-discovery.
